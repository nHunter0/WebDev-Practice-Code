Food Name Vitamins Why this food is healthy
Beans
  • Vitamin B1
  • Vitamin B6
  • Vitamin K
  • Vitamin B
  • Vitamin E
 Beans are both a nutritious and tasteful treat that work well with most any meal. Beans are a great source of fiber and helps regulate and protect your heart against high cholesterol.
Carrot
  • Vitamin A
  • Vitamin K
  • Vitamin C
 The fiber in carrots can help keep blood sugar levels under control. And they're loaded with vitamin A and beta-carotene, which there's evidence to suggest can lower your diabetes risk. Carrots can also strengthen your bones.
Orange
  • Vitamin C
  • Vitamin A
 Every type of orange has more than 100% of your recommended daily amount of vitamin C. That's more than any other citrus fruit. All you have to do to get this key vitamin is peel and eat.
Egg
  • Vitamin A
  • Vitamin B6
  • Vitamin B12
  • Vitamin D
  • Vitamin E
  • Vitamin K
 Eggs are a very good source of inexpensive, high-quality protein. More than half the protein of an egg is found in the egg white, which also includes vitamin B2 and lower amounts of fat than the yolk.
Avocado
  • Vitamin K
  • Vitamin C
  • Vitamin B5
  • Vitamin B6
  • Vitamin E
 This fruit is prized for its high nutrient value and is added to various dishes due to its good flavor and rich texture. Avocados are very nutritious and contain a wide variety of nutrients, including 20 different vitamins and minerals.
Milk
  • Vitamin A
  • Vitamin B12
 Milk is an excellent source of vitamins and minerals, particularly calcium. It has an important role in bone health. Nutritionists recommend that people have milk every day as part of a balanced diet.
Fish
  • Vitamin A
  • Vitamin B1
  • Vitamin B2
  • Vitamin B3
  • Vitamin B5
  • Vitamin B6
  • Vitamin B12
  • Vitamin D
  • Vitamin E
 Eating fish is an important source of omega-3 fatty acids. These essential nutrients keep our heart and brain healthy. Fish is a low-fat high quality protein. Fish is filled with vitamins and minerals such as D and B2.
Pork
  • Vitamin A
  • Vitamin B9
  • Vitamin C
  • Vitamin E
  • Vitamin K
 Pork is a rich source of certain vitamins and minerals your body needs to function, like iron and zinc. It's also an excellent source of high-quality protein. Minimally processed, lean, fully-cooked pork eaten in moderation can provide certain benefits when added to your diet.
Broccoli
  • Vitamin A
  • Vitamin B9
  • Vitamin C
  • Vitamin E
  • Vitamin K
 Broccoli is a good source of fibre and protein, and contains iron, potassium, calcium, selenium and magnesium as well as the vitamins A, C, E, K and a good array of B vitamins including folic acid.
Seeds
  • Vitamin B1
  • Vitamin B2
  • Vitamin B3
  • Vitamin B5
  • Vitamin B6
 Seeds are great sources of fiber. They also contain healthy monounsaturated fats, polyunsaturated fats and many important vitamins, minerals and antioxidants. When consumed as part of a healthy diet, seeds can help reduce blood sugar, cholesterol and blood pressure.
